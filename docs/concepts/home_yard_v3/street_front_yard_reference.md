# Street And Front Yard Reference

## Design Intent

The front of the home should establish arrival and real-world context before the race fantasy escalates. The street, curb, sidewalk, driveway, mailbox, porch approach, front planting, and property edge should make the home feel placed in a neighborhood rather than floating in a yard.

## Shape Language

Readable curb line, straight sidewalk with soft bends at the porch approach, driveway slab to garage or service side, low mailbox landmark, layered foundation planting, small path lights, and a porch threshold that points clearly to the home entry.

## Material Palette

Asphalt or dark street surface, concrete curb and sidewalk, pale driveway concrete, painted mailbox metal, mulched planting beds, green shrubs, painted front door, glass windows, and warm porch light.

## Gameplay Readability Notes

Front yard objects should frame arrival and route context without blocking racing sightlines. Driveway and sidewalk should be non-confusing surfaces distinct from the race road unless intentionally used by a course. Mailbox and porch approach are route-side landmarks, not collision traps.

## Asset Prompt Notes

Create low-poly curb/sidewalk/driveway/mailbox/porch-detail assets with clean suburban proportions, no text, no logos, simple collision, and strong silhouettes from player-height cameras.
